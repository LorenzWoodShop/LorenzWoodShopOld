Board Foot and Cost Calculator

When you buy lumber like hardwood usually you pay per board foot, which is a unit of volume.
One board foot is a 1 foot wide, 1 foot long, 1 inch thick volume, or 144 cubic inches.
Typically when you shop for wood you'll see something like Red Oak 4/4, that means it's 4 quarters of an inch thick.
Be sure to measure these boards as they are usually a quarter less from planing.
